Gardening Tips - Autumn

Plant Spring Bulbs

Easy

Now is the time to plant tulips, daffodils, and other spring-flowering bulbs. Plant at a depth of 2-3 times the bulb's height in well-drained soil.

Best done in early September

Prepare Your Garden

Medium

Clean up spent plants, add compost, and mulch beds for winter protection. Remove diseased foliage but leave some plant debris for overwintering beneficial insects.

Mid to late September

Harvest Remaining Crops

Easy

Pick remaining tomatoes, peppers, and other warm-season crops before frost. Green tomatoes can ripen indoors on a sunny windowsill.

Before first frost

Lawn Care

Medium

Aerate and fertilize your lawn to prepare it for winter and ensure healthy spring growth. Use a fall-specific fertilizer with higher potassium content.

Early September

Plant Cover Crops

Easy

Sow winter rye, clover, or vetch in empty garden beds to improve soil structure and prevent erosion over winter.

Mid-September

Seasonal Wellness - Autumn

Immune Support

Boost your immune system with vitamin C-rich foods as the weather cools. Consider adding elderberry, echinacea, and zinc to your routine.

Seasonal Affective Disorder

Prepare for shorter days by establishing good sleep habits and considering light therapy. Maintain vitamin D levels through supplementation if needed.

Outdoor Exercise

Take advantage of cooler temperatures for comfortable outdoor workouts. Try hiking, cycling, or outdoor yoga while enjoying fall colors.

Seasonal Nutrition

Transition to warming, nutrient-dense foods like root vegetables, squashes, and hearty grains to support your body through the season change.
